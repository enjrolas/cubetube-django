GETTING STARTED WITH YOUR CUBE

Introduction

Hello!  Welcome to the wonderful world of 3D LED volumetric display!  

If you’re here you’re likely one of the first owners of a L3D Cube  (8x8x8 edition).  Hi there you lover of the future, you!   This section of the site is intended to help you create and share apps on your Cubes.  Let’s get started!

Each L3D Cube 8x8x8 edition comes with 512 RGB LEDs mounted in an array, powered over USB (or via the L3D Cube Battery Shield), and connected to WiFi.  The brain and WiFi connection center of the Cubes is a Spark Core  (upgrading to the Photon in late June 2015), which can be  programmed like an Arduino.  There’s also a built in microphone and accelerometer in each Cube for music visualization and interactive apps.

If you’ve any questions that aren’t addressed here, head on over to the Forum or email Shawn & Alex at info@lookingglassfactory.com.  

Creating and Sharing on CubeTube

The magic of owning a Cube comes with being a part of the world’s first volumetric app sharing community, CubeTube!  Each Cube can download new apps from CubeTube.org with a click of a mouse or finger -- no programming needed.

But for any programmers out there - HELLO!  CubeTube is designed to help you create and share like a boss.  You can now write code directly in  CubeTube’s browser window, preview your programs in a simulated 3D Cube,  share the apps you create on CubeTube more easily than ever before, and fork other people’s code so you can build new Cube apps with startling speed.  More details in the CubeTube sections below!

8x8x8 Cube Kit Specifications

There are two types of L3D Cubes:  the 8x8x8 edition with 512 RGB LEDs and the 16x16x16 edition with 4096 RGB LEDs.  This documentation section currently only addresses the 8x8x8 edition and will be expanded to include the larger 16x16x16 edition in Summer 2015.

  • LEDs:  512 RGB surface-mounted LEDs.  The specific type of LED used is a WS2812B, made by WorldSemi.  There are 8 LEDs to each LED reed, and each LED reed is identical, connected into a custom base PCB.
  • Power: 5V 1A USB power can power most applications on your Cube.  We’ve also included a 2A  adapter. 100VAC - 220VAC input for higher power demand apps.  And there’s a bypass around the 1.7A current-limit we’ve built into the Cubes too, for ridiculously bright applications. 
  • Casing:  Laser-cut acrylic housing

Powering Up

Each L3D Cube comes with a 3-ft microUSB cable and 2A adapter.  5V, 500mA USB power can power the Demo Reel and many of the apps you’ll find on CubeTube - so your Cube can run off your computer, wall wart, or a battery pack. But we’ve also included the 2A adapter with 100VAC -  220VAC input for more intense visualizations (you will need to supply your own prong adapter if outside the US).

Note:  there is a 1.7A current limit built into the baseboard of the 8x8x8 Cubes.  There is a bypass built into the baseboard of the 8x8x8 L3D Cubes which allows advanced users to get around this current limit for super bright cubing.  Some of these modifications are popping up on the Forum, check them out!

PLAYING DEFAULT DEMO REEL

The 8x8x8 L3D Cubes come pre-loaded with four demos:

  • Fireworks
  • Zplasma
  • Squarral
  • FFT Joy (sound reactive)

The four demos will loop forever by default. You can stop the loop and play just one selected demo by tilting left, right, and forward.

If you don’t see the Demo Reel the first time you fire up your Cube, flip the switch at the back of your Cube to "Offline", and power cycle (i.e., unplug-replug power).

CONNECTING YOUR CUBE TO WIFI

Getting into Online Listening Mode

  1. To get your Cube connected to WiFi, first switch the Online/Offline slider switch at the back of your Cube to Online.

  • This may pause any programs running on your Cube.  Fear not.

  1. The LED on the Spark Core under your Cube should begin blinking blue.  If it doesn’t, push down the big "Join WiFi" button under your Cube.  Once the Spark Core is blinking blue, it’s in Listening mode, awaiting your WiFi credentials.

Using the Spark App to Load Your WiFi Credentials on your Cube

  • 3.  Since the Cubes use a Spark Core to connect to WiFi, you’ll need to download the Spark App to your phone from the App Store or Google Play to complete the connection process. (If you don’t have a phone that can download apps, you can still get your Cube online, it’s just a little more involved of a process to Connect over USB)
  • 4.  Once you’ve downloaded the Spark Core app from the App Store or Google Play, create an account.  This will also be the account you use for CubeTube.org.  Afterwards, you’ll be asked to connect your Core (which will thereby connect your Cube to your WiFi router).  
  • Note: Your phone must be connected to the  Wi-Fi network that you want to connect the Core to.  
  • 5.  When connected to Wi-Fi, the app will automatically fill in the SSID field with the name of the network that your phone is connected to.   Enter your Wi-Fi password and hit connect.
  • This connection process can take up to a minute. The closer your phone is to your Spark Core, the faster it will connect. Once the Core hears the signal, it will go through the following sequence of lights:
  • Solid blue: Credentials captured
  • Flashing green: Connecting to Wi-Fi network
  • Flashing cyan: Connecting to Spark Cloud
  • Breathing cyan: Connected to Spark Cloud
  • Once the Spark Core has connected, you’ll be asked to name your Core (and thereby also name your Cube).

NOTE: The Core MUST be online (breathing cyan)  in order for the claiming process to work. If the Spark Core has been claimed by someone else, the app won’t recognize it.

If you are connecting multiple Cores, you’ll go through this naming  process for each Core. You’ll know which one is which by the rainbow signal.

Now your Cube is online!

Switching to New WiFi Locations

When you move to a new WiFi locations, you can get your Cube’s Spark Core back into Listening Mode by clicking the big round "Connect to WiFi" button on the underside of the Cube.  This will not delete any programs you have stored in the internal memory of the Spark Core, and just clears the WiFi credentials and puts the Spark Core back into WiFi Listening Mode.  Remember, you’ll also need to switch your Cube into Online mode.  Then repeat the process above to connect to the new WiFi location.

CUBETUBE: THE VOLUMETRIC WONDERLAND

Loading CubeTube Apps onto Your Cube

When you first go to CubeTube.org, click the "Join or Log in" button.  Log in using the spark.io username and password that you created when you were connecting your Cube to WiFi in the previous step.

You can browse the Gallery and see simulations of most apps in your browser when you mouse-over the app thumbnail.

There are two main types of apps on CubeTube:

If you like a particular app and want to load it onto your Cube, choose one of your online Cube from the dropdown menu and click "Send to Cube".  If you chose a "Cube Required" app, immediately after you click the Send to Cube button, the LED on the Spark Core under your Cube should start to blink purple (indicating it is downloading the app).  This download process will take 10-45 seconds.

If you chose a "Cube + Computer Required" app for the first time, the L3D library will be sent to your Cube and the LED on the Spark Core under your Cube should start to blink purple (indicating it is downloading the L3D library).  After the download is complete, the app you see in the browser window should start in your Cube.    

Programming Cubes on CubeTube

BIG NEWS!  You can now write code directly in the browser window on CubeTube, fork other people’s programs (e.g., "Sketches"), and easily simulate and share your programs with the rest of the CubeTube community.

You can get started by forking another user’s sketch, or you can start from scratch by clicking the blue "Create" button.  To write code in Arduino language that will live entirely on Cubes (and won’t require a computer to run), choose the L3D tab.  To write Cube + Computer code that will require a WiFi-connected computer to run, choose the JS tab.

More details on writing code for the Cubes in the Docs  section of CubeTube.org.

TROUBLESHOOTING

Cube not Connecting to WiFi via Spark App

Connecting  your Cube to WiFi via the Spark App is the easiest way to get your Cube  online.  But it doesn’t work well with all routers.  As an easy first step, make sure your Cube is responding as expected by answering these questions:

  • Is your the slider switch at the backside of your Cube switched to Online mode?  
  • When you connect your Cube to power, does the LED on the Spark Core on the underside of your Cube turn on?  
  • Is that LED under your Cube blinking blue?

If you answered yes to these questions and you are still having trouble connecting your Cube to WiFi via the Spark App, don’t worry, we’ll get you online!  

First,  if you are technically inclined, try to claim a Core over USB via the Spark Command Line Interface.   

If that doesn’t work out, contact Alex and Shawn at  info@lookingglassfactory.com for help.  

And worst case scenario,  replacement Photons ship in late June, which will likely resolve  all connectivity issues.
